Why it matters

Why people want to hide apps on their iPhone

Privacy isn't just for people with something to hide — it's for everyone who believes their phone is their business. Maybe you're a hairdresser quietly building your own client list while still employed. Maybe you're a personal trainer who doesn't want your gym management to know you're planning to go solo. Maybe you simply don't want colleagues, family, or a nosy boss scrolling through your apps.

Apple offers limited native tools — you can bury apps in folders or remove them from your home screen. But none of that actually disguises what an app is. If someone looks, they'll find it.

Sidedoor takes a different approach: it lets you change the app icon entirely. So instead of a distinctive logo that gives the game away, Sidedoor can wear the face of a calculator, a weather app, a torch, or half a dozen other everyday icons. To anyone who glances at your phone, it's just another utility. Nothing to see here.

The disguise feature

10 icon options. One tap to switch.

When you set up Sidedoor, you choose which icon it wears on your home screen. Pick the one that best fits your phone — something unremarkable, something that won't prompt a second look. You can switch icon at any time from inside the app.

Beyond the icon, you also set a custom contact name. Sidedoor saves itself in your phone's contacts under whatever name you choose — Mum, a friend's name, your dentist. Anyone scrolling your contacts list sees a person, not a private networking app.

More than a disguise

Not a photo vault. A private professional channel.

Most "hidden apps" are photo vaults — places to stash pictures you don't want people to see. Sidedoor is something entirely different. It's a private contact and messaging channel designed for professionals who want to keep their client relationships completely separate from their employer.

The app works on a secret word system. You set a word — anything you like — and share it quietly with clients you want to stay connected with. They enter your word in the app, your number appears, and you're connected. No social media trail. No public profile. No way for your employer to know it's happening.
