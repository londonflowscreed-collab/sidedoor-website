The problem

Your contacts aren't as private as you think.

When you work with clients through an employer — a salon, a gym, a clinic — those client relationships often technically belong to the business. Your employer can see who you've been treating, training, or serving. If you leave, they can restrict your access to that contact information. Some contracts even prohibit you from contacting former clients at all.

And even setting contracts aside: your personal phone holds information your employer doesn't need to see. Every WhatsApp message, every saved number, every note — it's all there, and it's all yours. But employers can be intrusive, and platforms like WhatsApp blur the line between personal and professional all the time.

Sidedoor gives you a completely separate, private channel. Your clients connect to you directly — not through your employer, not through a platform that could be logged or audited. Your contact list is yours. Full stop.

The secret word system

A private door between you and your clients.

Here's how it works: you set a secret word inside the app — anything you like. You share that word quietly with clients you want to stay connected with. They download the app, enter your word, and your phone number appears. That's it. No accounts linked to your employer, no public profile, no trail.

Start fresh, cut ties, or move to a new employer — changing your word instantly locks out the old one. You're always in control.
