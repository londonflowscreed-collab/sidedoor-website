Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2025

The short version: we collect the minimum data needed to run Sidedoor, we don't sell it, we don't advertise with it, and you can delete everything at any time. The longer version is below.

1. Who We Are

Sidedoor is operated by Sidedoor (UK). We're a UK-based company and this service is subject to UK and EU data protection law, including the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

If you have any questions about this policy or how we handle your data, contact us at hello@sidedoor.app.

2. What Data We Collect

We keep this lean on purpose. Here's what we actually collect:

  • Account information: When you create an account, you provide either a phone number or an email address. That's it — no name, no photo, no social profile.
  • Secret word: Professionals set a secret word that clients use to connect. This is stored securely and used only to verify connections.
  • Connection events: When a client connects to a professional (or disconnects), we record that event — essentially a timestamped log of who connected with whom, using their Sidedoor IDs.
  • Subscription data: If you're a professional subscriber, we process payment through our payment provider. We don't store your card details — those stay with the payment processor.
  • Usage data: Basic technical information when you use the app — things like error logs and session data that help us keep the service running. We don't run analytics beyond what's needed for stability.

3. Why We Collect It

Every piece of data we hold has a reason:

  • Account info (phone/email): To create and manage your account, authenticate you, and communicate with you if there's a problem. Legal basis: contract performance.
  • Secret word: To enable the connection mechanism that is the core function of the service. Legal basis: contract performance.
  • Connection events: To show professionals who is currently connected, and to provide an audit trail. Legal basis: contract performance and legitimate interests.
  • Subscription data: To process payments and maintain your subscription. Legal basis: contract performance.
  • Usage/technical data: To keep the service stable and fix bugs. Legal basis: legitimate interests.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or anything beyond operating the service.

4. Who We Share It With

We don't sell, rent, or trade your data. Full stop.

The only third parties who touch your data are essential infrastructure providers:

  • Supabase: Our backend database and authentication provider. Your account data lives in Supabase's infrastructure. They process data on our behalf under a data processing agreement.
  • Payment processor: Handles subscription billing. They receive only what's needed to process payments and operate under their own compliant data practices.
  • Apple / Google: App distribution. They have their own privacy policies covering app download and device interactions.

We may disclose data if required to do so by law, regulation, or court order — but we'll tell you if we can.

5. How Long We Keep Your Data

We keep your data for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account:

  • Your account information (phone/email) is deleted immediately.
  • Connection event history is deleted within 30 days.
  • Subscription records may be retained for up to 7 years for tax and accounting purposes, as required by law. This is financial transaction data only — not your contact details.

You can delete your account at any time from the app settings.

6. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

As someone based in the UK (or EU), you have the following rights over your personal data:

  • Right of access: You can ask us what data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: You can ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure: You can ask us to delete your data ("right to be forgotten"). The easiest way is to delete your account in-app.
  • Right to portability: You can ask for a copy of your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Right to restrict processing: You can ask us to limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to object: You can object to processing based on legitimate interests.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at hello@sidedoor.app. We'll respond within 30 days. If you're not happy with how we handle your request, you can lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

7. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data from unauthorised access, alteration, or loss. This includes encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS), secure authentication, and limited internal access to personal data.

No system is perfectly secure, but we take this seriously. If we ever become aware of a data breach that affects you, we'll notify you as required by law.

8. Cookies

The Sidedoor app itself does not use tracking cookies. If you visit our website (sidedoor.app), we may use minimal session cookies necessary for the site to function. We don't use advertising cookies, analytics cookies, or any third-party tracking.

9. Children's Privacy

Sidedoor is not intended for anyone under 18. We don't knowingly collect data from minors. If you believe a minor has created an account, please contact us and we'll remove it.

10. Changes to This Policy

If we make significant changes to how we handle your data, we'll notify you via the app or by email before the changes take effect. The "last updated" date at the top of this page reflects when it was last revised.

11. Contact Us

For any privacy-related questions, data requests, or concerns:

  • Email: hello@sidedoor.app
  • Website: sidedoor.app
